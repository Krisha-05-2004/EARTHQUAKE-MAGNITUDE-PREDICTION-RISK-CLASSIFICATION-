Summary Key Findings
Regression Finding

Random Forest achieved strong R² of 0.75 and Linear Regression 0.66, confirming geographic and depth features are meaningful predictors of earthquake magnitude. Random Forest outperforms Linear Regression across all metrics — MAE 0.32 vs 0.38, RMSE 0.43 vs 0.50 — validated with 5-fold CV of 0.72 ± 0.04.

Classification Finding

Random Forest Classifier achieves 76% accuracy and Decision Tree 75.6% on risk level classification using only depth and coordinates — with no data leakage. CV accuracy of 76.45% ± 2.57% confirms the model is stable and generalises well to unseen data.

Feature Importance

lon_sin dominates both models as the most important feature, followed by latitude, lat_sin and longitude. This confirms that tectonic plate boundaries — which follow longitude-dependent curved patterns — are the primary geographic drivers of seismic risk.

Model Design Choices

Key design decisions: log-transforming depth (right-skewed), cyclic sin/cos encoding for lat/lon (avoids discontinuity at ±180°), stratified train-test split (80/20) for classification, and explicit exclusion of mag from classifier inputs to prevent data leakage.
